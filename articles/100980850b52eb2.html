      Buffalo ny driving ban

Updated on Nov 21, 2022 by Admin.

A travel advisory will be in effect in Erie County at 7 p.m. Thursday, but stricter measures may be implemented as a lake effect snow storm approaches.

During a Thursday afternoon news conference, County Executive Mark Poloncarz stated that if the conditions become too dangerous, plows may even pull off the roads, citing 4 to 5 inches of snow falling per hour.

According to a National Weather Service forecast, the first lake effect bands will arrive after 9 p.m. Thursday, with the most severe conditions in Buffalo and the Southtowns.

The National Weather Service predicts that the heaviest snow will fall late Thursday evening through Friday night, with snowfall rates exceeding 3 inches per hour.

Buffalo Mayor Byron Brown was also present at the press conference. He urged people to stay off the road, warning that getting stuck could be fatal.




Fifty crews from across the state have arrived in Buffalo and are ready to help when the snow begins to fall in Erie County on Thursday.

Poloncarz stated that a driving ban in Erie County could go into effect late Thursday or early Friday. He stated that tickets could be issued and that it is technically a crime to be on the road while a driving ban is in effect.

The commissioner of the New York State Department of Transportation added that the magnitude and speed of the snowfall in Western New York would make clearing roads difficult. More power crews are also on standby, anticipating regional outages.

"It will be a significant snow event, with snow falling at up to three inches per hour. On Friday, you should stay at home. If you can work from home on Friday, you should consider it a work from home day "Poloncarz stated this at a press conference on Thursday.
