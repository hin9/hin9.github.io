Updated on Nov 21, 2022 by Admin.

Erie County issued a travel ban beginning at 9 p.m. Thursday.

Prior to his 9 p.m. news conference, Erie County Executive Mark Poloncarz made the announcement on social media. At 8 p.m., he declared a State of Emergency, allowing the travel ban to take effect.

According to him, the travel ban "will be reviewed on Friday morning."

In advance of the lake effect snow storm, travel advisories have been issued for all of Erie County and a portion of the New York State Thruway.

New York Gov. Kathy Hochul declared a state of emergency in upstate New York on Wednesday.




A State of Emergency is currently in effect, with lake effect snow expected to impact portions of upstate New York through Sunday.

The New York State Thruway (I-90) is now closed to commercial traffic from exit 46 (Rochester I-390) to the Pennsylvania border. All commercial traffic on the Thruway heading east must exit at exit 61.

Commercial traffic traveling westbound on the Thruway from points east should exit at exit 46 (Rochester - I-390) for I-390 to I-86 West.

Officials request that you stay at home if you do not have to go out.
