Updated on Nov 21, 2022 by Admin.

Speaker Nancy Pelosi's tenure as leader of the House Democratic Party is coming to an end, and she is handing the baton to a new generation of leaders.

Why it is significant: Those who have stood by her side for nearly two decades, including Majority Leader Steny Hoyer and Whip Jim Clyburn, have said they are willing to step aside to allow the new guard to take power. Reps. Hakeem Jeffries (D-NY), Katherine Clark (D-MA), and Pete Aguilar (D-CA) have risen as a result.

What's making headlines: Pelosi announced Thursday afternoon on the House floor, in an emotional and historic speech, that she will not run for leadership again, but that she will remain a member of the House.

Steny Hoyer, an 83-year-old Maryland Democrat, has been coy for months about whether he would try to replace Pelosi as leader if she stepped down. But, like Pelosi, he announced on Thursday that he does not intend to seek another leadership position.

It is worth noting that other members may make their own bid to replace Pelosi, though this is unlikely. No one else has stepped forward to challenge Jeffries for the role as of yet.
