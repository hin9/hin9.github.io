      Did pelosi resign

Updated on Nov 21, 2022 by Admin.

On November 17, House Speaker Nancy Pelosi delivers remarks from the Capitol's House chamber.

Speaker Nancy Pelosi, who broke the "marble ceiling" to become the first woman to lead the United States House, announced on Thursday that she will step down as party leader.

"I will not run for Democratic leadership in the next Congress because I am confident in our caucus. For me, the time has come for a new generation to lead the Democratic caucus, which I greatly admire "Pelosi stated this during a speech on the House floor. "I'm grateful that so many people are ready and willing to take on this enormous responsibility."

Pelosi stated that she will remain in the House to represent her San Francisco district.

Pelosi warned in her remarks that democracy is "majestic, but fragile," and that voters in 2022 sent a message to Congress that they would not support those who supported violence or insurgency. She also praised the chamber for becoming more diverse during her 35-year tenure. There were 12 women in the Democratic caucus when she first entered Congress in 1987; now there are 90. "And we want more," she applauded.

Her decision comes a day after Republicans officially took control of the chamber in the 2022 midterm elections, and three weeks after her husband, Paul, was violently assaulted at their San Francisco home.

After the "red wave" failed to materialize on Election Day, the GOP is expected to have a razor-thin majority. Democrats defied historical expectations by outperforming expectations in governor, Senate, and House elections.




Her decision comes as no surprise on Capitol Hill, and it is consistent with a promise she made four years ago to self-term limit after Democrats won the majority in 2018 and she became the first speaker to claim the speakership twice since the legendary Sam Rayburn. There has been a quiet desire among rank-and-file Democrats to elect a younger slate of leaders to replace Pelosi, who is 82, and the two other top House Democratic leaders, Reps. Steny Hoyer, D-Md., and Jim Clyburn, D-S.C., both of whom are in their 80s.

Following Pelosi's announcement, Hoyer, the current House Majority Leader, announced that he will not run for leadership in the next Congress.

"Now is the time for a new generation of leaders," Hoyer said in a statement, adding that he would support Democratic Rep. Hakeem Jefferies in his bid for the presidency.

House Democrats will hold leadership elections at the end of the month, and Pelosi's announcement is expected to spark a rush of leadership bids.

Jeffries of New York, Katherine Clark of Massachusetts, and Pete Aguilar of California are at the top of the list. All three are currently in lower-level leadership positions and want to advance. Jeffries, Clark, and Aguilar, all 52, would make an African American, a white woman, and a Hispanic the party's new faces. Rep. Adam Schiff (D-Calif.) is also considering a leadership run.

Even those who have previously disagreed with Pelosi praise her record of steering her often divided caucus through difficult times with little room for error.
